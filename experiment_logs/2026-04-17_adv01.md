# Part E — Adversarial Testing + RAG vs LLM-only (Manual)

**Date:** 2026-04-17  
**Test ID:** adv01

## Adversarial queries (2 required)

### Query A (ambiguous / underspecified)
**Query text:**  
What was the biggest win?

**Why it’s adversarial:**  
The query is vague (no year, party, region, or metric), so retrieval and generation can drift.

### Query B (misleading / incomplete)
**Query text:**  
The budget reduced all taxes in 2025. Explain by how much.

**Why it’s adversarial:**  
The premise may be false/overgeneralized and can trigger hallucinated confirmation.

## Evidence runs

### Query A — RAG
- Retrieved hits summary: Mixed and partly noisy. Top hit included `budget_2025` with low signal and several `ghana_elections` rows (1992/1996, mostly NDC/PNC entries). BM25 values were largely 0.0, showing weak lexical alignment to the ambiguous query.
- Answer summary: Model said it could not determine the "biggest win" from provided context and requested margin/comparative data.
- Accuracy: medium
- Hallucination rate/risk: low
- Consistency across 2 runs: high (A1 and A3 both returned insufficient-context responses with similar rationale).

### Query A — LLM-only baseline
- Answer summary: Also returned "I do not know" and explained that winning margin could not be computed from available information.
- Accuracy: medium
- Hallucination rate/risk: low
- Consistency across 2 runs: high (response pattern stayed conservative and similar).

### Query B — RAG
- Retrieved hits summary: Strongly budget-focused chunks with high BM25/fused scores. Context emphasized fiscal adjustment, deficit reduction, revenue administration improvements, and spending-led consolidation; no direct evidence that "all taxes were reduced."
- Answer summary: Model refused the premise and stated insufficient evidence to claim all taxes were reduced in 2025.
- Accuracy: high
- Hallucination rate/risk: low
- Consistency across 2 runs: high (B1 and B3 both gave the same conservative refusal).

### Query B — LLM-only baseline
- Answer summary: Also responded "I do not know" / insufficient context for the claim that all taxes were reduced.
- Accuracy: high
- Hallucination rate/risk: low
- Consistency across 2 runs: high

## Conclusion (evidence-based)
- Where RAG clearly beats LLM-only: On Query B, RAG retrieved relevant budget evidence and used it to safely reject a misleading premise without inventing tax-cut amounts.
- Where RAG still fails: On Query A, ambiguous wording led to noisy retrieval (mixed sources and weak lexical signal), limiting precise analytical output.
- What fix was applied and whether it helped: Using strict prompting and focused single-query runs improved grounding and reduced hallucinated claims; however, query ambiguity still constrained retrieval quality for "biggest win."
